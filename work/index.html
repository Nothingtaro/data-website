01 Professional · Shopee · 2023–2025

Payment-Operations Automation at Shopee

Python automation for the prepaid-campaign payment workflow on Shopee's BD Infrastructure team, built to take repetitive manual steps off the payment-solutions team.

Problem

Prepaid-campaign payment operations ran on repetitive manual steps that were slow and easy to get wrong, and the volume grew with every campaign. The team needed the throughput without adding headcount.

Method

Gathered requirements from the payment-solutions team, mapped the manual workflow end to end, and built Python automation for the repetitive steps, with validation checks so accuracy held up as volume scaled.

Result

Cut prepaid-campaign task volume by 70%, freeing the team for higher-value work and shortening turnaround while keeping error rates low.

Limitation

The automation was scoped to the prepaid-campaign flow and its specific rules. Cases outside that flow still needed manual handling, and the logic had to be revisited whenever campaign rules changed.

What I'd change

Generalize it into a configurable pipeline so new campaign types onboard through config rather than code, and add monitoring that flags rule changes instead of relying on someone noticing.

02 Senior project · 2023

Deep Learning for Thai Food Image Classification

A convolutional neural network that identifies Thai dishes from a single photo, built and benchmarked across four architectures for my Statistics senior project at Chulalongkorn University.

Problem

Thai dishes look alike and go by many names, which makes them hard to identify from a photo, especially for visitors ordering food. Could a model do it reliably from the image alone?

Method

Applied transfer learning across four CNN architectures (VGG16, ResNet50, InceptionV3, and Xception) under two schemes: feature extraction and fine-tuning. Trained on 3,600 images spanning 12 Thai dishes (Kaggle), resized to 224×224, with data augmentation, dropout, and early stopping. Python and TensorFlow/Keras on Google Colab.

Result

The fine-tuned Xception model reached 93.06% accuracy (93.02% F1), the strongest of the four, classifying dishes such as Pad Kra Pao at near-100% F1.

Limitation

Scope was 12 dishes from a curated dataset, so the model isn't validated on dishes outside those classes, mixed plates, or the lighting and angles of real ordering photos. The remaining errors sit between visually similar dishes.

What I'd change

Widen the class set, fold in real user photos, and look at calibration and the confusion between lookalike dishes rather than headline accuracy, then export a quantized model for on-device inference.

03 Competition · 2022 · Team of 5

Dynamic Vehicle Routing · EURO Meets NeurIPS 2022

Dispatch strategies for the vehicle routing problem with time windows, built for the EURO Meets NeurIPS 2022 competition as part of the five-person Team4sake.

Problem

In the dynamic setting, customer requests arrive throughout the day. At each epoch you must decide which requests to dispatch now and which to postpone: a bet that shapes total routing cost.

Method

Starting from the Hybrid Genetic Search static solver, we designed and compared five dispatch strategies: a distance Cutoff, a Modified Cutoff that grows per epoch, a Centroid Modified Cutoff, a logistic-regression Ensemble that selects the best strategy per instance, and a single-customer postponement rule. Features were engineered from instance geometry: customer density within distance percentiles.

Result

The cutoff-family strategies beat the greedy, lazy, and random baselines. Team4sake placed 26th of 54 teams in the dynamic setting (static cost ≈180,843; dynamic ≈360,945).

Limitation

Cutoff thresholds were tuned on the competition's instance distribution, so they wouldn't necessarily transfer to different demand patterns or epoch lengths. Placing 26th of 54 also means a good number of teams' policies beat ours.

What I'd change

Learn the dispatch policy directly, for example with reinforcement learning over epochs, instead of hand-set cutoffs, and validate it on out-of-distribution instances rather than only the competition set.
